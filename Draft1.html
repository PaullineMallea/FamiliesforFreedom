How a United States District Court Circumvents Oversight of Unlawful Detention.

Habeas Corpus

A writ of habeas corpus, is a legal instrument used to bring a prisoner before the court to determine if the person's imprisonment by the ICE is lawful.

Importance

The writ of habeas corpus lies at the heart of society’s protection against the illegal deprivation of liberty by a ICE.

Fame

The writ was most famously used in 1772 by Lord Mansfield to liberate a Jamaican slave who was forcibly brought to the United Kingdom, paving a legal path to the abolition of slavery.[1]

[1] Sommerset v. Stewart

  1. Habeas Corpus Filed

    6 Months

    Once an immigrant has been detained for six months after being ordered deported, their detention becomes unlawful. Immigrant detainees then seek relief by filing a habeas in federal district court - only if the government can justify their detention should it continue.

    In the Northern Disrict of Alabama:

    95%

    of habeas corpus petitions filed in the Northern District of Alabama are filed pro se, meaning immigrants are writing and filing these petitions without a lawyer.

  2. Court Order

    What should happen:

    3 days

    Court sends the government (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) an order to respond within 3 days.

    What is happening:

    30 days

    The court automatically gives the government (ICE) 30 days to respond.

    ​

  3. Extension

    What should happen:

    The court can only give an extension if the government (ICE) shows good cause.

    What is happening:

    The court always grants an extension to the government (ICE).

    ​

  4. Hearing

    What should happen:

    The court orders a hearing.

    What is happening:

    The court has not granted a single hearing.

    ​

  5. Denial

    What should happen:

    The court examined the justification and orders release where detention is unlawful where detention is unlawful because the government has not justified further detention.

    What is happening:

    Out of more than the 240 habeas petitions filed the Northern District has not granted a single Habeas corpus petition since 2010.

    ​

  6. Fact

    No Habeas Corpus has ever been granted in 5 years.
