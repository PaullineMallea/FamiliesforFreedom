Habeas Corpus

A writ of habeas corpus, is a legal instrument used to bring a prisoner before the court to determine if the person's imprisonment by the government is lawful.

Importance

The writ of habeas corpus lies at the heart of societyâ€™s protection against the illegal deprivation of liberty by a government.

Fame

The writ was most famously used in 1772 by Lord Mansfield to liberate a Jamaican slave who was forcibly brought to the United Kingdom, paving a legal path to the abolition of slavery.[1]
